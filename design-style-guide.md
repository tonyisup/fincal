# AI-Assisted Design Style Guide
*Based on design review principles for AI-built landing pages.*

## 1. Avoid "AI Slop" & Ubiquitous Trends
AI models are trained on popular sites, leading to repetitive design choices. Avoid these "tells" that signal your site was generated rather than crafted:
- **Overused Palettes:** Avoid default purple gradients or "Google-y" primary color palettes (red/yellow/green/blue) unless they are core to your actual brand.
- **Excessive Animation:** Just because you *can* code a complex animation doesn't mean you *should*. If it doesn't add value (like explaining a complex product workflow), remove it.
- **"Dumb" Hover Effects:** Avoid hover effects that disappear, shift elements weirdly, or move buttons away from the cursor. Stick to standard, intuitive interactions.

## 2. Information Hierarchy & Readability
AI often generates "busy" pages that lack clear flow.
- **Simplify Hierarchy:** Don't mix too many font styles or unnecessary labels. Keep it clean.
- **Prioritize the Fold:** Above the fold must clearly answer: *What is it? Who is it for? Why does it matter?* Plus a clear Call to Action (CTA).
- **Contrast:** Ensure text is readable against backgrounds. If a dynamic background (like a video) is used, ensure the menu text remains legible at all times.

## 3. Functionality > Flash
An interactive site should not be a "game" if it compromises usability.
- **Avoid Scroll-Jacking:** Never hijack the native browser scroll or force users through "molasses-like" animation sequences.
- **Don't Hide Critical Info:** Never rely on "hover" to reveal essential product functionality or navigation. Assume users (especially on mobile) won't see it.
- **Obvious Interactions:** Make interactive elements look clickable. If a user has to "hunt" for what they can click, you’ve lost them.
- **No Moving Targets:** Never have buttons or UI elements chase the cursor or shift position unexpectedly.

## 4. Quality Assurance (The Human Loop)
AI produces "good enough" drafts, not polished products. You must be the editor.
- **QA Everything:** AI often leaves bugs (layout shifts, broken animations, unresponsive elements). Test your site on multiple screen sizes.
- **Asset Quality:** Check for blurry images or low-res artifacts. Your site should always look high-resolution.
- **Be Opinionated:** Don't just click "Accept all changes." Be the human judge. If the AI suggests a weird hierarchy or a strange layout, override it.

## 5. Purpose-Driven Design
Your landing page is a **Customer Acquisition Channel**, not an art project.
- **Originality is Credibility:** Customers are becoming savvy; if your site looks identical to every other "AI-generated" site, they will question the quality of your underlying product.
- **Start with Brand, Not AI:** Feed your own color palette, brand identity, and messaging *into* the tool, rather than letting the tool generate a generic brand for you.
